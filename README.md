The labor market is a vital part of the economy, playing a key role in promoting economic growth, improving people's living standards, and facilitating international integration. In the context of increasingly deep international economic integration and the strong impact of the Fourth Industrial Revolution, studying and analyzing the Vietnamese labor market is extremely important.

This report will focus on analyzing the Vietnamese labor market during the period from 2018 to 2023, including:

• Overview of the Vietnamese labor market.

• Income.

• Skill levels of the workforce.

• Strengths and weaknesses of the Vietnamese labor market.

This project demonstrates visualization tools in Power BI.
Dataset:
The data for this analysis was obtained from the General Statistics Office website (https://www.gso.gov.vn/).
1. Analysis of Labor Force (2018–2023)

<img width="675" height="573" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/428cbe8e-cdf1-450d-a568-dc82403a9e3a" />

The total labor force fluctuated during 2018–2023, declining significantly from 56 million to 51 million workers during 2019–2021 due to the COVID-19 pandemic, before recovering from 2022 onward.

Male workers consistently accounted for a higher proportion than female workers throughout the period, with only slight fluctuations of around 1–2%.

By 2021, the proportion of male workers had increased compared to 2018, while the proportion of female workers had decreased.

-> Conclusion: The labor force was negatively affected by COVID-19 but began to recover after 2021, while male workers continued to represent a larger share of the workforce than female workers.

2. Analysis of Employed Workers by Area

<img width="707" height="637" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/100c3ef1-3178-459e-b827-c9ac76fe1716" />


- The number of employed workers in rural areas showed a slight decline during 2018–2020, followed by a modest increase during 2021–2023.

- The number of employed workers in urban areas increased steadily throughout 2018–2023.

- In 2023, there were 32.3 million employed workers in rural areas compared to 19 million in urban areas.

- Overall, rural employment tended to decrease, while urban employment continued to rise.

-> Conclusion: The trend indicates an ongoing shift of labor from rural areas to urban areas, driven by urbanization and economic development.

3. Analysis of Unemployment Rate

<img width="642" height="360" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/ac552f37-8106-4c83-9f9c-4ee99387d041" />
<img width="705" height="267" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/1c5c3ee9-9364-4648-8423-e5d8c0baddcb" />

The overall unemployment rate increased from 2018 to 2021 and then gradually declined in 2022 and 2023.

Female unemployment was generally higher than male unemployment, reaching a peak difference of 1.04% in 2020.

Urban unemployment remained consistently higher than rural unemployment, peaking at 2.1% in 2020.

The unemployment gaps between genders and between urban and rural areas narrowed in 2022 and 2023.

The rise in unemployment during 2020–2021 was largely caused by the economic disruptions of the COVID-19 pandemic.

The subsequent decline reflects economic recovery and the normalization of business activities.

-> Conclusion: Unemployment increased during the pandemic but declined as the economy recovered, with disparities across gender and location gradually narrowing.

4. Analysis of Workers’ Income

<img width="705" height="267" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/be8a3c1b-0e55-4fe0-b05e-b52d126f9e20" />

Workers’ average income generally increased during 2018–2023, despite declines in 2020 and 2021 due to the COVID-19 pandemic.

Male workers consistently earned higher incomes than both the national average and female workers.

Female workers’ income remained below the national average throughout the period.

As the economy recovered in 2022 and 2023, workers’ incomes rebounded and exceeded pre-pandemic levels.

-> Conclusion: Workers’ income showed long-term growth despite temporary declines during the pandemic, while income disparities between male and female workers persisted.

5. Analysis of Trained Labor Force by Educational Qualification

<img width="672" height="475" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/16052ca9-fb94-4eef-a9c6-de93c7a9f8fc" />

The proportion of trained workers in Vietnam increased steadily from 22% to 27% during the period.

The shares of workers with elementary vocational training and university degrees or higher showed a consistent upward trend.

The proportion of workers with intermediate-level qualifications declined over time.

The proportion of workers with college-level qualifications fluctuated throughout the period.

-> Conclusion: Vietnam's labor force has become increasingly skilled, with growth in workers holding vocational and higher education qualifications, although changes across educational levels have not been uniform.
